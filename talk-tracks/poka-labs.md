## 2. THE PROBLEM

{#990000}**"Commercial leaders at specialty manufacturers and distributors we talk to usually tell us:"**{/}

• **Pricing requires assembling fragmented data** across systems — past quotes, product specs, cost sheets, customer history — plus expert judgment. It's slow and inconsistent.

• **Sales teams bottleneck** waiting on technical or pricing experts — 3-day quotes lose deals to faster competitors.

• **Quotes are inconsistent across reps** — same product, different prices depending on who quotes it.

• **Win rates suffer** because slow, inaccurate quotes lose deals before they even get to negotiation.

• **No learning loop** — pricing knowledge stays siloed in experts' heads. When they leave, the knowledge walks out the door.

• **Price books make quoting easy but pricing rigid** — can't adapt per customer, per deal, leaving margin on the table.

• **Dynamic pricing takes too much internal time** — quotes still go out late.

• **Existing customers keep getting the cheap "acquisition price" forever** — nobody raises prices because nobody knows where to raise them.
