## 4. QUALIFYING QUESTIONS

*{#999999}Ask these to determine fit. You need at least one strong positive signal to proceed.{/}*

### Must Ask

{#990000}**1. "Do your recruiters interface with hundreds of candidates each month? How do they manage those candidates today?"**{/}

*{#999999}Yes, plus signals of manual repetitive work (emailing, phoning, trawling LinkedIn, updating spreadsheets) → these all indicate processes Popp can automate.{/}*
*{#999999}No high-volume candidate operations → DISQUALIFY. Popp needs volume to deliver value.{/}*

### Nice to Ask

{#990000}**2. "Are there areas of your recruitment funnel that could be optimised so recruiter time goes to revenue-generating activities?"**{/}

*{#999999}Acknowledgement that time is being spent on non-revenue tasks → same signal as Q1, phrased differently. Use whichever lands better.{/}*

{#990000}**3. "Are all parts of your business performing to their full potential, or is there room for improvement?"**{/}

*{#999999}Indicators that certain divisions, desks, or geographies are underperforming → prime use cases for Popp.{/}*

{#990000}**4. "What ATS do you use?"**{/}

*{#999999}Knowing the ATS helps shape the discovery call. Don't force it — only ask if natural.{/}*
